Summary: Are meteorologists making up new terms for sleet and hail? While hail is normally associated with thunderstorms and sleet consist of melted snowflakes that refreeze into hard pieces of ice, graupel is just a little different. When snowflakes find themselves among traces of supercooled water, the water adheres to the ice of the snowflake, freezing instantly. What you see falling are little snow pellets which you can squish with your fingers. While hail can cause damage, sleet is just little pieces of hard ice, while graupel are little snow pellets.
