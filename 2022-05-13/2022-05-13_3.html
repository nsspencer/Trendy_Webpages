Senior Year review – Rebel Wilson fails Netflix high school comedy

Summary: The spirit of sparky high school comedies, from Never Been Kissed to Mean Girls to Easy A, haunts Netflix’s junky “if you like…” offering Senior Year, with a progressively deafening reminder of what came before. For where those films had charm, wit and vim, this has a stultifying absence instead, a disappointing and derivative two-hour slog down memory lane. There’s proven comic mileage in comparing the nature of high school life then and now, something 21 Jump Street handled well, the leads forced to reconfigure their ideas of popularity and how to wear a backpack. There’s no attempt to add any real humour or any kind of inventive spin to the performance, it just … is. Senior Year might get a passing grade for sheer energy but for everything else, it’s a fail.
