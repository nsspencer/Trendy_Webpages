When was the last “White Christmas” in West Texas?

Summary: SAN ANGELO, Texas — White Christmas and west Texas are not words you typically see in the same sentence unless it regards the lack of White Christmases in the area, however, there have been a few occasions west Texans have witnessed the magic of a snowy Christmas. Official snowfall data exists for Abilene and San Angelo, the two largest cities within the NWS San Angelo County Warning Area. This was also the last White Christmas Abilene has seen since. As for San Angelo, “a White Christmas has not been observed since before Irving Berlin ever put his famous song into words,” said the NWS. The NWS calculated the probability of each location having a White Christmas, as slightly above 2% in Abilene and just under 2% in San Angelo.
