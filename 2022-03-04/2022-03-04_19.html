Chris Olave, Garrett Wilson run 40-yard dash in under 4.4 seconds at NFL combine

Former Ohio State wide receivers Chris Olave and Garrett Wilson both clocked 40-yard dash times under 4.4 seconds at the NFL scouting combine in Indianapolis. The official times were 4.38 seconds for Wilson and 4.39 seconds for Olave, making them the seventh and eighth-fastest receivers who ran on Thursday night. ESPN reported they’re only the second pair of teammates since 2006 to post sub-4.4 second times during the same combine. In 2019, Parris Campbell ran the 40-yard dash in 4.31 seconds, and Terry McLaurin finished in 4.35 seconds. Baylor wide receiver Tyquan Thornton had the fastest 40-yard dash time this week at 4.28 seconds.

Chris Olave, Garrett Wilson Show Why They’re First-Round Picks With Standout Performances at NFL Scouting Combine

It took less than 4.4 seconds for Chris Olave and Garrett Wilson to solidify their status as first-round draft picks at the NFL Scouting Combine. Combining what they did in receiver drills with their 40 times, their draft stocks should have only improved on Thursday night. Although there were seven other wide receivers who also broke 4.4 in the 40, Wilson and Olave were the fastest of the wide receivers who have been widely projected as first-round picks. While Wilson beat Olave by just one one-hundredth of a second on the official 40 times, Olave beat Wilson by just one inch in the broad jump, leaping 10-foot-4 to Wilson’s 10-foot-3. If they weren’t already both locks to be selected in the first round of the 2022 NFL draft on April 28, they should be now.

Did Ohio State's Chris Olave just run away from the Raiders at pick No. 22?

INDIANAPOLIS — The Raiders are always in the market for speed at the receiver position and had to be a little excited about the prospect of landing a fast, polished route runner like Ohio State’s Chris Olave with the 22nd pick in the NFL Draft next month.That possibility might be left in the dust after Olave was one of eight receivers to run under 4.4 seconds in the 40-yard dash at the NFL Scouting Combine at Lucas Oil Stadium on Thursday night.The polished Olave was thought to be a late first-round pick before he ran an unofficial 4.26 and had NFL Network analysts howling. Whether or not they were in on all of the unofficial times being a lot faster than what seemed logical, Olave’s amended official time of 4.39 was still the eighth-fastest time of all the receivers.For someone who might be the best route runner of the bunch, Olave caught practically every pass thrown his way during drills, easily tracking deep throws. He measured in at 6-feet and 3/8 inches and 187 pounds with a 31 1/8-inch arm length and 73 1/8-inch wingspan.“I just want to prove it to myself,” Olave said Wednesday when asked what he wanted to accomplish here. “I’ve been training for this for so long, and to get this once-in-a-lifetime opportunity is a huge blessing.“People can see how fast I can run on film, but to showcase that in front of all the scouts and all the teams is going to be huge for me this week.”One wonders whether Olave would have passed on a second 40 attempt if he knew the first time was so far off. But NFL Network analyst and former Jaguars and Raiders running back Maurice Jones-Drew was on the field and said Olave still verified what everyone thought coming in.
