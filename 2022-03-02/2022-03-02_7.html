SMU Catholic Campus Ministry Celebrates Mardi Gras – SMU Daily Campus

The Catholic Campus Ministry filled their living and dining area with gold, purple and green to celebrate Fat Tuesday with their annual event, Mardi Gras at the C, on March 1st. Beads, feathers and masks adorned the tables and walls of the Catholic Center to fit the festivities. Since Mardi Gras is heavily celebrated in Louisiana, the entrees were cajun delicacies like jambalaya and gumbo. Mardi Gras is seen in the Catholic faith as a day of full celebration before the season of Lent starts the next day. The Catholic Campus Ministry is open to all SMU students, and many have found joy in taking part in events just like these that the organization hosts.

Winners chosen for 8th annual King Cake Snob contest

(BRPROUD) — Just two bakeries swept all five categories in the 8th annual King Cake Snob contest, organizers announced Wednesday. Susan’s Sassy Sweets, a newcomer to the competition based in Bay St. Louis, Mississippi, took the title of Best Traditional King Cake and Best Freshness. Claudia’s King Cake, a bakery located just outside New Orleans in Harahan, won Best Filled King Cake for the 5th year in a row. Claudia’s was also won Best Presentation and Best Flavor. King Cake Snob says it received over 1,200 reviews for 330 king cakes during this year’s competition.
