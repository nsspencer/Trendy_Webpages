Belarus authoritarian leader Alexander Lukashenko gains more powers in Belarus after referendum

Belarusian President Alexander Lukashenko has secured further power for himself in a referendum. The amendment will allow Lukashenko, who has ruled the country since 1994, to secure further time in office and guarantee him lifelong immunity from prosecution after his eventual withdrawal from politics. It would also allow Russian troops and nuclear weapons to be permanently stationed in Belarus in future. The move comes as a senior US intelligence official is reported as saying Belarus is expected to send troops into Ukraine as soon as Monday to fight alongside Russian forces. The official told Associated Press the decision by Lukashenko on whether to bring Belarus further into the war depends on talks between Russia and Ukraine in the coming days.
