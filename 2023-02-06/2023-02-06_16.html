Explore Harriet Tubman’s Life and the Underground Railroad in Maryland

Summary: Take a road trip to Maryland’s Eastern Shore, and get an in-depth look at Harriet Tubman’s life. The country roads leading to the Harriet Tubman Underground Railroad Visitor Center cut across shallow marshlands filled with cattails, scrubby pine, and cedar trees. Tubman traversed this landscape when she led 13 expeditions on the Underground Railroad, a secret network of safe houses and operatives who worked to emancipate enslaved people from the American South. Photo courtesy National Park ServiceAfter watching a short film, visitors can view exhibits highlighting milestones in Tubman’s life with objects to touch, sound stations, and recreations of her heroism.
