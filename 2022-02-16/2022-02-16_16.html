Frank Pesce, Character Actor in ‘Beverly Hills Cop’ Films, Dies at 75

Frank Pesce, a character actor whose credits included the first two Beverly Hills Cop movies and such TV shows as Miami Vice, Who’s the Boss?, Jake and the Fatman and Karen Sisco, has died. Born in New York on Dec. 8, 1946, Pesce worked as an actor for more than four decades. His most recognizable roles came in the first two Beverly Hills Cop films that starred Eddie Murphy. Pesce also played a bartender in Top Gun (1986) and a wiseguy in Donny Brasco (1997). Pesce had a story credit on 29th Street (1991), a film about a very lucky man named Frank Pesce Jr. (Anthony LaPaglia) and his rather unlucky father, Frank Sr. (Danny Aiello).
