Afghanistan: Incidents affecting Humanitarians (January - November 2022) - Afghanistan

Summary: KEY ISSUESHumanitarian assistance to 22.1 million people in Afghanistan, continues to be delivered in a volatile environment that directly affect the humanitarian operation, and risk the safety and security of aid workers and assets. As of November 2022, 532 incidents of serious threats and risks against aid workers and operations were reported. These incidents involved 362 instances of 'violence against humanitarian personnel, assets and facilities', followed by 99 incidents relating to 'restriction of movement of agencies, personnel, or goods within the affected country', and 70 incidents of 'military operations and ongoing hostilities impeding humanitarian operations'. Physical violence against aid workers (99 incidents reported), including arrests, harassment/ intimidation, injuries and death, was of a key concern to aid workers. Majority of the incidents affecting humanitarian operations occurred Kabul (144), Kandahar (52),Nangarhar (46), Hirat (39), and Kunduz (25) provinces, mainly resulting from criminal activities (27 per cent), and security threats (20 per cent).

Afghanistan begins last phase of polio vaccination

Summary: Kabul, Dec 19 (EFE).- The Taliban began the last phase of its polio vaccination campaign in Afghanistan on Monday, seeking to immunize nearly seven million children, concluding a year in which only two cases of the virus were recorded across the country. “The ministry of public health of the Islamic Emirate of Afghanistan – as the Taliban call themselves – in cooperation with relevant international agencies today launched the last round of polio vaccination campaign this year,” the ministry spokesperson Sharafat Zaman Amar told EFE. Afghanistan and neighbouring Pakistan are the only countries in the world where polio is endemic. In this regard, the authorities called on the families of the minors to collaborate with the vaccination teams in help erradicate the disease from the country. Polio vaccination teams have often been subject to attacks, including one in February this year in which eight workers were killed in northern Afghanistan, leading to the United Nations office in the country to stop vaccination in two provinces with immediate effect.
