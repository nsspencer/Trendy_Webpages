This week’s snowfall has been record-breaking in Michigan’s U.P.

MARQUETTE, MI – It’s been a record-breaking couple days in Michigan’s Upper Peninsula. According to the National Weather Service in Marquette, several snowfall and precipitation records were broken on Monday and Tuesday. The snowfall broke a record 19.4 inches the area received on Feb. 26, 2002. The previous record for snowfall on Feb. 22 was just 7.5 inches in 1974. “While the previous records were on the lower end, we did set new precipitation and snow records the last two days.
